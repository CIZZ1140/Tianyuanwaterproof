## Product Overview
Thermoplastic Polyolefin (TPO) single-ply waterproofing roofing membrane. This advanced formulation combines ethylene-propylene rubber (for elasticity and durability) and polypropylene (for strength and weldability). It is modified with stabilizers, antioxidants, and UV absorbers to resist extreme weather, making it highly suitable for exposed roofing projects.

## Key Benefits
- **Cool Roof Efficiency**: High solar reflectance index (SRI) which reflects solar heat, contributing to building energy savings.
- **Extreme UV Resistance**: Excellent resistance to ultraviolet light, ozone, and thermal aging.
- **Eco-Friendly**: Environmentally friendly: recyclable, plasticizer-free, chlorine-free, and non-hazardous.
- **Welded Seam Integrity**: Seams are hot-air welded to create a cohesive, leak-proof, monolithic joint stronger than the sheet itself.
- **Flexibility**: Remains flexible at low temperatures (-40 deg C) with high puncture resistance.

## Applications
- Exposed commercial and industrial flat roofs
- Pitched roofs, residential light-gauge steel roofs, and concrete decks
- Refurbishment and retrofitting of worn-out metal roofing systems
- Swimming pools, artificial ponds, and underground civil structures

## Installation Method
TPO can be installed using three main systems: 1) Mechanically Fastened: The membrane is secured to the deck using specialized plates and fasteners through the edge, and overlapping sheets are hot-air welded. 2) Fully Adhered: Substrate adhesive is applied to the deck and sheet backing, mated together, and rolled, followed by hot-air welding of seams. 3) Loose-Laid and Ballasted.
