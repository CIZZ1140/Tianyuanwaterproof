## Why Choose PVC for Critical Engineering?

PVC waterproofing membranes are the smart choice for modern infrastructure. Unlike traditional bitumen products, PVC remains flexible for decades and uses **thermoplastic welding** to create an inseparable bond. At Tianyuan Waterproof, we ensure our PVC meets global engineering standards for durability and environmental resistance.

### 1. Internal Reinforced Structure (The Secret of Strength)

The reliability of a PVC membrane starts from within. Our **Type P (Reinforced)** series is engineered with a high-tenacity polyester fabric core.

*   **Internal Polyester Scrim**: Acts as the "skeleton" of the membrane, preventing stretching and providing exceptional tear resistance.
*   **UV-Resistant Top Layer**: Specifically formulated to reflect solar heat (Cool Roof effect) and resist degradation from long-term UV exposure.
*   **Durable Bottom Layer**: Optimizes adhesion to various substrates while maintaining overall sheet flexibility.

![PVC Internal Structure Schematic](https://img.tyuanwaterproof.com/products/pvc-structure.png)

---

### 2. High-Precision Installation: Monolithic Seams

The weakest point of any waterproofing system is usually the joint. PVC solves this through **Hot-Air Welding**. By melting the top and bottom layers of adjacent sheets together at 450°C+, they fuse into one single, continuous piece.

*   **No Adhesives Needed**: Unlike EPDM or bitumen, the joint itself becomes the strongest part of the system.
*   **Automatic Welding**: Our membranes are designed for high-speed automatic welding machines (like Leister), ensuring consistent quality across large-scale projects.
*   **100% Watertight**: Once cooled, the seam is monolithic, meaning it will never delaminate or leak even under standing water.

### 5. Technical Comparison: Choosing Between H, P, L, and G Types

| Type | Name | Structure | Typical Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Type H** | Homogeneous | Pure PVC sheet | Tunnels, underground, and detail applications |
| **Type P** | Reinforced | Internal Polyester Scrim | **Exposed roofing (Mechanically Fastened)** |
| **Type L** | Fleece-backed | Fabric/Fleece Backing | **Fully Adhered systems** and renovations |
| **Type G** | Glass Fiber | Internal Glass Fiber | Systems requiring enhanced dimensional stability |
